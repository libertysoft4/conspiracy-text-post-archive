Regarding the "flat earth" conspiracy - who benefits?

0  2017-03-11 by xereeto

I'm sure there are a couple flat-earthers in here, and I'm just looking for a proper answer to this.

All major conspiracy theories have one thing in common.

  • The Illuminati are engaged in mind control to establish the New World Order, and use hidden symbols to communicate.

  • Roswell was covered up to prevent mass hysteria.

  • JFK was assassinated by the CIA because he wanted peace with Russia.

  • The Beatles secretly replaced Paul McCartney with a lookalike because they were afraid they would lose fans without him.

  • NASA faked the moon landing to beat the Soviets in the space race.

  • The AIDS virus was created in a lab to reduce world population.

  • Elvis Presley faked his own death to escape his fame.

  • The Royals killed Diana because she was an adulterer.

  • Bush did 9/11 so he could invade Iraq.

  • The concept of global warming was created by and for the Chinese in order to make U.S. manufacturing non-competitive.

Each one of these, regardless of whether it's true (I don't believe any of them are) or even scientifically plausible (the moon landing one definitely isn't), at least makes sense from a motivational standpoint. Someone gains from the deception. So my question is, why would every country in the world take part in this massive conspiracy that ultimately gains nothing? It makes literally no difference to anyone whether the Earth is round or flat, so what is the incentive to lie?

The theory perhaps had some credibility when it was simply a difference of interpretation of facts. "No, reality doesn't fit your model, you are wrong". Fine. But in the rocket age, it's no longer "you're wrong"... it's "you're knowingly lying to us".
