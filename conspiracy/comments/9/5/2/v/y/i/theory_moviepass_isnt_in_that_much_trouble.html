Theory: MoviePass isn't in that much trouble

1  2018-08-06 by 48LawsOfFlour

There's a recurring narrative in the media and the tech media that MoviePass is on the ropes. MoviePass is a service where you can see movies on a recurring basis for cheaper, kinda like a subscription to the movie theater.

I don't think MoviePass has financial troubles. I think they have a good PR team.

Uber treats its cash flow issues as kind of a dirty secret. It's maybe every fifth or tenth story. MoviePass's supposed financial impossibility manages to make the focus of every single story about them.

What's the message being communicated here? MoviePass is a good deal! It is passing on so much value to its customers that it's almost dying. Furthermore, this may just be a limited time offer-- MoviePass might not exist tomorrow. Buy MoviePass now!

It's a mattress store commercial. At the top of TechCrunch and other rags again and again.

No thanks, MoviePass's PR team. I'll enter in to collective bargaining when my job is on the ropes, not when the movie industry can't think of any more good ideas.
