The Trump administration abruptly canceled a planned FBI headquarter move, years after plans had been started and made. The plan was to move from a downtown location to a suburban location. The administration's excuse that this contract was 'a bad deal,' and that the original location could be demolished and rebuilt for cheaper. Conveniently, the existing FBI headquarters is right across the street from a Trump Hotel, which sees significant amounts of FBI-related business. It turns out that the administration's plan to completely rebuild instead of move was lowballed. An internal investigation into this matter (due to the potential conflict of interested) revealed that his administration officials gave intentionally misleading information as to how much influence Trump had on this decision.

This week the Inspector General (IG) of the General Services Investigation (GSA) issued a report which found that, despite previous denials, President Trump was personally involved in the decision to overturn years of planning for the relocation of the FBI headquarters. That decision has a significant impact on Trump’s financial interests, given the proximity of the current headquarters to the Trump International Hotel.
