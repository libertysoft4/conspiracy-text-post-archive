What difference does a One-World-Government or 197-world governments make if the rules or law are well-made?

1  2018-07-20 by jay_howard

To the anarchists and the anti-one-world-government proponents, how do you live in peace? I cherish the freedoms I have also. But unless you're truly "off the grid" (which very few humans do, despite their claims), you can't afford to enforce every contract you enter into. I certainly can't.

If you can't count on contracts being enforced, the economy gets paralyzed out of fear. Everyone becomes a thief when the justice system disappears. I have my issues with authority like anyone else--including authorities--but I'm not foolish enough to want to live in a world without them.

So here we are: not quite a 1WG, but international trade trends ever toward it. Sure, there're going to be several iterations, variations of trading partners and spheres of economic security, but something like a 1WG will eventually win out.

I've heard a 1WG was a terrible thing for literally decades. Alex Jones and others shout their protests against the "Amero" (unified North American currency) every now and then. But the size of the state in question, it's simply irrelevant to me. Am I under-reacting?

What seems exponentially more important are the rules that the state must follow and the legal mechanisms that regulate state powers. Shouldn't the rules of the state take precedence over the size of the state?
