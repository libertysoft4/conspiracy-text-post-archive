Same with 9/11: either you believe the official conspiracy theory or one of the alternative ones.

It would be more beneficial to the group (of humans, collectively), if each of us could sort through an actual theory to see if it met fairly simple criteria of a "good" theory:

  • fasifiability, or testability: if a theory cannot be demonstrated to be false--even through a thought experiment--chances are it's not a good theory

  • coherence: if a theory contradicts itself, it's crap

  • explanatory power: given 2 or more competing theories, the one that can explain the most evidence/phenomena without having to dismiss it as "unreal" or "insignificant" is the better theory
