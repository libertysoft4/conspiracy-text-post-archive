Consider your monitor - each "pixel" is actually made up of three physical pixels, one red, one green, and one blue. If each of those pixels was changed to be white light only, you could have a monochrome monitor with three times the definition without having any more physical pixels.

It would be black and white, but much higher definition.

That's what they do with the sensors on Hubble. The increase the definition by only having pixels that detect the intensity of any colour of light, but then put filters in front of it. When they use a green filter they get a green image with three times the resolution.

By taking three separate images with red, green and blue filters, then combining the three images together, they get a full colour image that is three times the resolution than if they had used separate R,G,B pixels to take one full colour image.

What do you think the scientists prefer? Higher definition but composited photos, or lower definition photos where all the colour is captured simultaneously?
