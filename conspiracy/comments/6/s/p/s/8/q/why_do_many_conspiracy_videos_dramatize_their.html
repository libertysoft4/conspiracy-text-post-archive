Why do many "conspiracy videos" dramatize their content so much with soundtrack and effects?

151  2017-08-10 by GoTomArrow

This is actually the top reason I don't watch conspiracy videos anymore. They all (sorry) use "super ominous" music and scary sound and visual effects I find it hard to focus and not have my brain drift into a "dumb consumer" mode. In a way, I feel about them like I feel about most TV shows; like they're trying to bypass my objective viewpoint and emotionally manipulate me.

Incidentally most of them also tend to present rather weak arguments, so I kinda suspect many of them to be ... how do you say ... well, not actual conspiracy videos, but stuff meant to catch curious minds and detract them into harmless nonsense.

I wish somebody did documentaries and inquisitive shows that aren't overly dramatized. Yeah I know Hollywood does it too. And I wish they'd stop. But only a fool would hope for that. Hollywood makes it's money from skillfully shutting off the mind and sometimes that can be enjoyable (although barely), but that's not what I expect from a show that is supposed to be at least a little inspiring.

A good show I like, for example, is David Attenborough's "Life". Why? Well: The soundtrack tends to be minimal and it is edited in a way so that you have enough time to follow and grasp all the concepts that are explained and think critically about them. It is structured well. It also doesn't overdo special effects and doesn't cut too often so your eyes/ears don't get overburdened with stimuli (which would also detract from any critical and conscious approach). Pacing. It is edited by someone unafraid of letting a picture sit there for a while and letting you take it in.

I mean, if you talk with a real person in real life, do you just hammer them with arguments and ideas and give them no time to respond and think? Nope.

TLDR: Whenever I see a conspiracy video that starts with scary music and zoomed in slow motion videos with Brightness -50 and Contrast +50 and a lot of shock-y sound effects, I just feel that something is a little off. And when I say "a little", I mean "all my red flags go up".
