Journey to secure my Internet has lead to some surprises

131  2017-05-26 by TimeBandit88

This won't be short, but I'll try to be on point, and I'm not going to fill in every blank. I'm not providing links. This was my journey. I attest to truth within. If you want TLDR, look elsewhere. This is for thinkers and those who care about privacy.

This all started, because a couple years ago I noticed a lot of my traffic going through akamaitechnologies.com. It turns out that this is one of a few major companies responsible for publishing "The Internet" for us consumers along with amazonaws, 1e100.net (google), and others.

Every search I look into on this gives the same dead end which is: If you block them most of the Internet won't work, so don't worry about it. They're just hosting copies closer to you to help reduce traffic bloat.

Note that I am here posting this with full blocking in effect. Many sites are inaccessible, and for this I am thankful. You will see why.

I had let all this slide until recently. With it being legal for a certain 3 letter group to hack anyone in the US as of last December, my paranoia got piqued. That alone didn't push me into action, but it did cause me to watch my netstat more frequently.

It was FireFox that really got me going. I check my netstat regularly, and I saw that it has two localhost connections upon loading. No big deal right? Of course the public Internet and the mozilla forum says this is no big deal. It's basically a necessity due to Windows not handling Unix code as well.

I did find one conspiratorial post out there though, calling this a stack intercept. It got me wondering about Odin's Eye, and how that alleged secondary processor would be able to intercept traffic without some method of communication with the primary CPU.

After some more digging I stumbled upon a little program called TCPView. I live by this now. It is everything you see in netstat, but real time. You see a lot more.

With this I began to see FireFox making active connections upon loading it, even if I didn't go anywhere.

Some of this I was able to attribute to add-ons, particularly NoScript due to some advanced settings (I can elaborate on this if asked), but there were connections occurring independently of all that.

The main culprit was the site of map2.hwcdn.com which is owned by Highwinds Content Delivery Network - another one of those companies responsible for publishing "The Internet", and is apparently owned by Akamai. Full circle now. Coincidence?

Going a step further, Highwinds is based out of Phoenix, Arizona. Ever heard about those massive data centers in the AZ desert having to do with Echelon? I'll bet it's these guys.

I've proceeded to block out every IP block owned by every snooping corporation that has been cited thus far in my post with my router access list. This can be confirmed by connections marked as SYN_SENT instead of ESTABLISHED.

It doesn't stop here though. Upon review of my router's IP connection tracker, I found more established connections even when getting a fresh IP address, and not accessing anything.

So it seems my router may also be hacked, even though it is a security focused firewall router.

Here are the IP addresses that it showed as being connected to at some point even when not using the connection.

216.99.133.23 is the most persistent offender. It pops up with some regularity. I couldn't trace it to anything in particular, but the nearest IP that I could resolve to it was 216.99.131.3 which is in03.sjc.mx.trendmicro.com.

TrendMicro, OK that sounds familiar. Apparently it's a multinational security company based out of Japan. Very interesting.

The next unsoliticed IP I came across was from my ISP in a neighboring state. I've seen that one a few times as well. Neither of these IPs seem to change either.

The third and most recent was 216.218.206.114. It took me a minute to figure this out, but it's in the same IP block as www.startpage.com the alleged safe and private search engine recommended by Snowden, who was obviously in my mind co-opted long before Assange was last year.

startpage.com resolves to 216.218.239.62 and 216.218.239.142. Not a dead ringer, but darn close.

You might say, but you went to that site purposefully, and that was true, but that was before I changed my IP address the last time (change router's MAC address clone while modem offline to do this) which suggests that startpage hacked my modem simply by going there, even with all this other IP blocking going on.

Getting back to the big companies delivering the Internet to us, I went through the trouble of pinging every single OCSP site (for publishing certificates which handle trust relationships on your browser), and about half of them resolved to Akamai. I suspect this is a means of certificate hijack for compromising operating systems.

I can only hope that only allowing connections to valid sites going forward will do the trick. There's not much else I can do besides reformat everything.

Finally, I seem to be at the point where both TCPView and my router logs indicate no unsolicited connections, but I am not confident that will remain the case.

If you are not security conscious, accept that you are most likely compromised.

I'm happy to provide my block list if anyone is interested.
