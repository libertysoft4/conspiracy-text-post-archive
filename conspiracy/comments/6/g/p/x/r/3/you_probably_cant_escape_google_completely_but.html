I even tried putting hardware firewalls on non-intel chipsets (building them with openwrt or using specifically designed firewall oses) to put a physical layer in between my box and the internet. Disabling wifi/bluetooth (by physically opening the machines up and removing the radios) and using ethernet only. Even "safe" oses like openbsd would get compromised quickly. All of that went away when I went on Qubes. The added benefit was that if a vm smelled of compromise I could shutdown the vm. There were a bunch of different tactics that could be used to reduce the compromise smells with Qubes that I couldn't find anywhere else. For instance keeping a vm that's completely airgapped and using the Qubes interdomain copy mechanisms to move files from the vms that had network stack.
