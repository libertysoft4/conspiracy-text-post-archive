But...the curvature of the earth defines the visible distance across the surface of water. Water isn't actually flat over a long distance. You can tell this by calculating how far away the horizon is when you are in the middle of the ocean.

For an observer who was theoretically standing on the surface of the water with height of 5 ft 7 in (average eye-level height), the horizon is at a distance of 2.9 mi. You can't see further than that because the earth curves and the water level actually dips underneath what you can see and it is obscured by the other water.

For someone standing a little bit higher, say on a 3-4 passenger boat, eye level would be around 6 ft 7 in, the horizon is at a distance of 3.1 mi. The reason for this is that you are starting higher up, so you can see further before the curvature of the earth takes the water out of your field of view.

For an observer standing on a cruise ship tower or something like a oil drilling platform with a height of 330 ft, the horizon doesn't fall out of view until 22 mi away.

The formula to calculate this (in standard units not metric) is SquareRoot(height above surface / 0.5736) = distance to horizon

So for someone whose eyes are at 5.5 feet tall the calculation would be SquareRoot(5.5 / 0.5736) = 3 miles.
