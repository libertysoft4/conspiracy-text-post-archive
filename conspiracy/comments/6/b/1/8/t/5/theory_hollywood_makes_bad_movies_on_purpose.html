Theory: Hollywood Makes Bad Movies On Purpose

3  2017-05-14 by benedictFocker

Basically, Hollywood's goal is to make a revenue stream. If they make too many good movies people will get desensitized.

Instead, they make occasional good movies to create a sense of magic, so that any movie might be that really good one. Then they stick to formula.

The formula is designed to draw fans inspired by the good movie back to the theatre time and again until they are burnt out. Hollywood doesn't use good movies to do this but little demographic appeals and focus group tested stunts (certain demographics that could spend money likely on tickets love car crashes, for example).

But movies themselves tend to suck because the goal is to get you to the theatre enough to squeeze money out of you.
