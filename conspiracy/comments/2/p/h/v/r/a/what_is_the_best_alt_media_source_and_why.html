I was asked this yesterday, pasting response.

There's no one answer, you'll have to find your own. Personally for hard news facts-only coverage a mix of reasonable mainstream sources work for me - BBC/FT/France24/Euronews, avoiding stuff like CNN or Al-Jazeera and government mouthpieces like Voa/RT/PressTV.

Hard to find an alternative source of news that isn't trying to convince me of something at the same time.

The best way to learn is curated variety.

Read a little bit from many outlets. Do not trust an outlet that uses buzzwords or loaded language.

Don't mistrust everything, be open to new ideas but not bullshit. If it walks like a duck don't call it Fido.

Never mistake opinion for news, keep them separate. Never trust anything that is written like a press release from Pyongyang.

Avoid outlets that claim to have SOLVED or UNCOVERED something SHOCKING. Don't click on anything with SMOKING GUN in the title - it won't be.

In general if something has NEVER BEFORE BEEN SEEN there's a good reason - it's irrelevant or invented yesterday.
