Even more prevalent than directly paying off scientists, which likely does exist to a degree, is the way that peer review and academia works.

In order to continue to hold a research professorship, you must publish, preferably in the prestigious journals in your field. To get published in these journals, your paper must be accepted by those journals' editors. If they don't like your research or its implications, they can simply reject the paper.

The whole 97% climate consensus is based on published papers. Who's to say how many papers that disagreed with that consensus were rejected from the journals? It's a lot easier to buy off a few journals than all the scientists in a field.
