Your first statement isn't correct. Here is a simple home made visualization. The problem with the videos you're citing is that you have no frame of reference for what "level" is. All you have is where the center of the frame is, but that's not proof of level, that's just where the camera is pointing.

You have to be careful with gyroscopes. They don't always maintain their orientation. You have to account for all forces acting on them, like the force the case they're in exerts on them to keep them from bouncing against their surroundings. When working with things like angular momentum, the moment of objects becomes much less intuitive, as you have to account for things like torque and angular momentum, not just regular linear forces.
