Infinite space isn't quite the same thing as infinite possibility of everything occurring in the same infinite space.

It's a safe assumption that the laws of chemistry are uniform throughout the universe, even if the universe is actually infinite. So there aren't going to be e.g. any plutonium-based lifeforms.

Same goes for the laws of physics, although in fairness it has to be said that there is a lively debate going on about whether the laws of physics that we're familiar with are only "localised" (it's a completely hypothetical/philosophical argument, mind you, with no actual evidence to back it up.)

So the boring likelihood is that any space-faring intelligence will be pretty much the same as us. Walking meatsacks, travelling along inside a sealed capsule so they don't die in the vacuum of space.

A few advance starships full of robot soldiers would be the way any sensible black-hat ET would tackle a planet that s/he/it fancied depopulating and colonising.
