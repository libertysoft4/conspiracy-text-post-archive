It's unlikely that you will get any sort of satisfactory answer here, because of how the human brain works.

The human brain does not like to be told its wrong. There's an enormous amount of study behind the idea that people become wedded to their beliefs--especially if they feel they have 'searched' them out.

If evidence appears to the contrary of these beliefs, people are likely to *refuse to see it, *alter it to match their beliefs or *only see the part of evidence that -already- matches their belief.

So if someone--anyone--has pursued what they feel is a journey to a 'truth' (in this case a conspiracy theory, but it can be any 'belief/truth'), the chances of them suddenly reversing course is very, very small. It's also why all over the internet, people attempt to point out things (on any subject, on any side) and just get yelled down by those who oppose what is being pointed out.
