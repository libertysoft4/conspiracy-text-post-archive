67  2018-02-27 by c0mput3rn3rd

Let me preface this by stating I hardly use any social media. It creeps me out so I only use Facebook, because I am required to manage the club at my college I am president of, and Instagram because of my photography freelance work. I would ditch even these if i could. Also worth noting I'm rather careful with what my apps have permission to use.

But I digress.

The first time this happened I had a long conversation with my Dad one night about different colleges and options for my future. My phone had been sitting next to me, unused for the duration of this conversation. Afterwards I pulled up Instagram only to see an advertisement for one college my dad had kept bringing up throughout the night.

The second time I noticed this was after a phone call with my dad. We had been discussing an upcoming project we are planning, a digital night vision system, and discussed how we also needed a helmet to mount it on. After the phone call and again on Instagram I saw an advertisement for a guy using an armored helmet with night vision mounted on it. The ad was specifically for the helmet, which is also the one part we haven't ordered yet.

I wanted to share not only to see others thoughts but also to perhaps bring some more awareness to how far Big Brother actually reaches. It is far outside just the government.

TL;DR: My phone listened into "private" conversations and displayed targeted ads to me.
