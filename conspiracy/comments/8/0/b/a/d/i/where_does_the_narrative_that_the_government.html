Where does the narrative that the government needs to outlaw guns to subjugate its people come from?

9  2018-02-26 by screeyonk

On a law-enforcement level, police have no problem abusing the rights of citizens, and those who defend themselves are both few and far between and rarely successful. In the case of deliberate ramping up of laws and police enforcement, gun ownership is a fairly minor obstacle. I might even argue that in the 'big picture' armed resistance would be beneficial, since a rising police state could paint its agents as martyrs fighting against a violent element in society.

In regard to weakening potential large-scale rebellions, the idea that a world power could be overturned by a violent civilian coup has been fairly unlikely for decades. While it would certainly be easier to defeat a rebellion if they had to rely exclusively on foreign-sourced or stolen weapons, automatic small arms just don't seem to me to pose a serious enough threat against a modern mechanized force.
