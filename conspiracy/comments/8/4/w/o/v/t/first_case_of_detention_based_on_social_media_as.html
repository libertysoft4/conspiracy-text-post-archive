Sheriff Israel has received much media coverage for his calls for police to have authority to make mental health diagnoses necessitating detention and treatment, instead of mental health professionals, as is currently the case.

He also calls for power of preemptive detention based on "disturbing" social media posts, which will be determined at police discretion, but which he has mentioned may specifically include imagery or mention of "guns", rifles", "bombs", or "blood".

News story released yesterday:

Sister Of Charleston Shooter Dylann Roof Arrested After Menacing Social Media Post

Dylann Roof's sister, Morgan Roof, was arrested based on a social media post criticizing the student walkout, wishing violence upon the participants.

However notably, as stated by the school principal, the post was "hateful" and "inappropriate", but "not a threat".

Based on the post, she was arrested.

Her belongings were then searched, to reveal pepper spray, a knife, and pot. She was charged with possession of weapons on school property.

The governor has stated that a "potential tragedy was avoided".

This story very neatly justifies the recent calls for detention based on non-threatening, but "disturbing" social media posts.
