The more worrying issue ... is its use of digital information consumption as a psychological control vector.

I would propose that such a control mechanism is already within reach, on reddit at least. When you read and post material to an anonymous forum like reddit and many others, you have to just assume that your fellow comment-posters are indeed flesh and blood like the tangible human being you meet in the park or the grocery store.

Online interaction leads down a ramp towards "hybrid-AI-human" intelligence.

Level 1: Malicious disruption. A troll is simply a real person acting badly - calling everybody names and trying to jack around with whatever the topic is so nobody can feel good about it, except maybe the troll. But sometimes a troll has ulterior motives. There are just plain vandals. Then there are neighborhood enforcers that "vandalize" whoever the boss doesn't like...

Level 2: Mercenary misdirection. A shill is worse, because they are doing a job. Like mercenaries, they are doing the dirty work without too many questions about why and who gets hurt.

Level 3: Automated misinformation. An AI bot is the worst, especially a really good one, that has been trained by some kind of linguistic GAN so it seems like it actually follows the 20-reply conversation it just had with a persistent redditor... These ones are amazingly convincing within certain domains, and they no longer need an attendant to swoop in and make some clumsy excuse to close out the entirely fake conversation.

Level 4: Fractal guidance. Coming next, if not now, is the bot-dominated fractal forum, where each participant reads a different set of posts and replies. So if there are 10 posters, for each one the AI presents a separate set of replies. They all think they are talking to each other, but they are actually talking to an individualized subprogram which decides what messages are passed to which forum members.
