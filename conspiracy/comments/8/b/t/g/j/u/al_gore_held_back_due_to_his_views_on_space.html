The signal is bounced between those satellites until it reaches the one above White Sands in New Mexico. If any of those antennas are misaligned the signal could drop. If anything passed between those satellites and interrupted the signal, the signal could drop. If a significant weather system lay between the last satellite and White Sands, the signal could drop. A Solar event could cause the signal to drop. A power fluctuation in the ISS or any of the relay satellites could cause the signal to drop.

Being able to aim a radio signal at the Earth is not enough. That's not how it works anymore.
