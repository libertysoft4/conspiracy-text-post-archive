(many researchers are not hopeful research will reveal new information) The National Institute for Justice released the 2018 solicitation which includes Investigator Initiated Research and Evaluation on Firearms Violence, slated for May 2018.

  2. -eliminate absurd restrictions on the ATF- this calls for a digitization of records by the ATF, which is already in place. Within the ATF they maintain several databases with information (NTC) some of that information includes sales reports, as of 2011 they had roughly 4.2 million of such records (even though the National Firearms Act forbids a keeping of a registry). This request also calls for annual inventory checks of gun dealers. As part of federal law the ATF may do compliance once per year, currently they are only conducting annual inspections on roughly 7% of the dealers.

  3. -universal background checks- 20 states currently require background checks on either handguns or long guns or both in private sales. US Federal Law currently requires all purchasers of gun sales from federally licensed dealers to undergo a background check.

4.-High Capacity Magazine Ban- There are currently 7 states that ban high capacity magazines. The 1994 congressional ban on magazines capable of holding more than 10 rounds of ammunition expired in 2004 and was not renewed. Currently, high capacity ammunition magazines are legal unless banned by state or local governments, 7 such states do have bans in place for said magazines.

5.-Limit firing power on the streets- The 1994 federal Violent Crime Control and Law Enforcement Act did ban assault weapons, that law also had a sunset clause and was not renewed in 2004. It is now up to the states to ban such weapons.

It would appear that all of these goals have been addressed by individual states as well as the nation as a whole.
