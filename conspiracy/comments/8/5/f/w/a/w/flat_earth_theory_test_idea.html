Flat Earth Theory Test Idea.

0  2018-03-19 by Kabukikitsune

As we all know, from time to time (seems to come in waves) flat earth theories pop up here every so often. There have been multiple attempts, using math and various other equations to both prove, or disprove the theory, all with rather...well mixed results.

The more I've thought about this particular theory, the more I think that there would be a somewhat easy (if costly) way to really settle the argument once and for all.

Here's the test I propose, please tell me what your thoughts on the subject are.

First you acquire (likely rent) a pair of identical airliners which have enough range to cross the Atlantic.

Second, you would need an airport with two parallel runways.

Third, you would need a pair of crews and a control group (scientific meaning) of syncronized time pieces.

The experiment:

Both planes are given a full load of fuel, and take off at the same time, or at the very least within minutes of each other. One crew flies a straight path with minimal deviation from London to NY, or vice versa. The second flight uses the usual curved path that cross atlantic flights use, again with minimal deviation.

Here's the payout, so to speak, for the experiment.

If the world is round, then the flight taking the second path should take roughly 8 hours to transit the path (give or take 1 hour due to weather or deviations), while the straight path should take roughly 14 hours (give or take an hour).

Alternatively, if the earth is flat, then the flight times would be reversed; with the straight path plane arriving first, and the curved one being the later one.

As to the control group of time keeping, each plane carries syncronized clocks to verify that they did not overly deviate from their flight paths. IE, the straight path doesn't circle somewhere over the ocean to make the flight take longer.

Thoughts?
