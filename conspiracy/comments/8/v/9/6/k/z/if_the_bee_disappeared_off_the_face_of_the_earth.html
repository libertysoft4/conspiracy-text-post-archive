We first removed predators from farms/livestock/living areas to protect against attacks from them. While it allowed livestock/people/property to be safer it had an unintended consequence of removing a population control feature of prey animals. We next went after pest animals that had reproduced to extraordinary levels thanks to the above predator removal. This was to prevent them from eating the seeds, vegetables, etc... from farms. While it did work it too had numerous consequences that we didn't understand or think would happen. The animal pest removal had unintentionally also targeted non-pest animals which frequently ate pests i/e plant eating insects. This increased the number of insects and necessitated the need for the next issue.

When the insect population grew drastically as they could breed/reproduce unhindered or controlled, their damage to food production did as well. This caused us to create more deadly/efficient pest control chemicals to deal with the problem. These chemicals too killed indiscriminately and so non-pest insects(pollinators and even insects which preyed on pest insects) were equally effected. It also caused mutations to appear in pest insects(not just ones that eat plants but sucking/biting insects that preyed on animals) which caused them to become much harder to kill. Eventually these chemicals stopped being as useful. This growth in insect populations also effected non-agricultural regions and so insect levels were huge all over.

These species had further devastating effects on native animals specifically fish and other water based animals. As with the zebra mussel invasion and numerous others, the invasive species reproduced drastically which starved native species of oxygen which caused mass die offs of fish which was very prevalent in the late 20th century. The die offs caused a further increase in insect populations as the population in fish/other animals that ate them or their eggs/babies lowered. The invasive species sometimes would not eat insects so their benefit to the environment was generally negative.

The way we sprayed also changed as well. A new approach was found in which insects were targeted where they bred as well as where their higher numbers were found. Here is a good video which discusses this concept through Disney's implementation of it. This lowered the amount of pesticide used which again lowered run off.

That run off had unknowingly harmed animal species through various means which lowered their populations as well so the controlling efforts allowed a rebound effect of animal life. This population rebound caused a ripple effect which is discussed in this video.

Pest animals were also better controlled/targeted which allowed the beneficial pest eating animals to no longer be killed. An example would be how birds are dealt with around airports.
