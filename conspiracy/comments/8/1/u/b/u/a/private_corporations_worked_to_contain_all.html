I think in these discussions there tends to be a lack of nuance, where people either decide YT is perfectly within their rights and should not be criticized, or that YT is wrong and therefore we should have government intervention.   1. YT is likely acting within the law in censoring who they are (i.e. not violating the 1A or public accommodation laws)

That means choosing services like BitChute or DTube over services like vimeo.

We have a few options: put public pressure on YT to get them to change their policies (unlikely to work), put public pressure on the government to change the law or enforce laws already on the books (likely to have very unwanted side effects), or use alternative forms of communication.
