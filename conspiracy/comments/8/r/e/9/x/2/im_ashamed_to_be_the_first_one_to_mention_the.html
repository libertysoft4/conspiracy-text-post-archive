1  2018-06-15 by ThatClownFromIt

It's essentially a U.K style blanket ban on all substances that have psychoactive effects comparable to any scheduled substances. They use vague legalese statements such as...

  • Any "chemical structure that is substantially similar to the chemical structure of a controlled substance in schedule I, II, III, IV, or V; and"
  • "an actual or predicted stimulant, depressant, or hallucinogenic effect on the central nervous system that is substantially similar to or greater than the stimulant, depressant, or hallucinogenic effect on the central nervous system of a controlled substance in schedule I, II, III, IV, or V;"

It seems like pretty average totalitarianism that gives them the right to ban every unscheduled analogue of an illegal drug and has effects comparable to one (Caffeine would fall in this category as being stimulating in the same way Cocaine and Amphetamine is). Where they crossed the line and completely disregarded any attempt of reasonably constructing the legislation is they used the word PREDICTED in reference to the effects a chemical has.

Who gets to decide what a prediction is? Can Jeff Sessions just wake up and decide Vitamin C might be psychedelic and schedule it? Can he bump his head and become deluded to the level where he can become convinced that any random substance might have some sort of no-no effect and schedule it? Can an average shitposting troll make a fancy looking blog article about why some random compound might be able to do something like a drug and influence the DEA/FDA to prohibit it for the lulz?

Where does this shit end? Because a blanket ban on Research Chemicals seems like it should be the end of it, but who would be able to predict if they'll eventually get inspired to crack down on gardening supplies?
