when people asked for the original film material that was brought back from the moon NASA said they have lost it.

Fyi: this is incorrect. NASA still has the still photos and video film taken on the moon, they were never lost.

What was lost/destroyed was the live analog broadcast as it was received in Australia. There were two types of video camera on the moon, the 16mm Maurer film camera and the television broadcast camera. The television camera only worked at 10 frames per second, and in order to convert it live from transmission to the US (the radio signal was received in Australia because the landing happened at night), the only technology available was to use a kinescope, which was a 30 fps camera pointed at a 10fps tv monitor.

The original broadcast was recorded and NASA had copies, but they recorded over them in the 80s due to a tape shortage. The 10 fps/low resolution video wasn't considered to be important scientifically, so it was low priority for preservation.

The Mauser camera film is still available, same with the photographs, just not the slightly higher quality original live television broadcast.
