Lots of details about this seem pretty crazy, but I wonder specifically about the questions in my title.

Finances

  • Paddock was a defense contract auditor then went to work for what is now Lockheed and Martin. Idk about his Lockheed job, but Glassdoor has senior defense auditor salaries at $80k which isn't huge.

  • He worked for the gov't for 9 years and Lockheed for 3 years before going into real estate

  • After selling his company and retiring he considered gambling his retirement profession

It seems like Paddock didn't have lavish expenses, but a 12 year career in professions that aren't highly paid (the exception potentially being Lockheed) doesn't seem like enough to begin a successful career in buying and renting real estate. I'm sure he could have taken out loans and he was an accountant so obviously he was smart with money, but he owned a lot of property across the country which seems like he'd need some serious capital for.

It's going to be interesting if details come out about his gambling earnings/losses. Casinos track everything so the info is all there somewhere. Was he winning or losing and what were the numbers? There is a company called Central Credit that keeps track of gamblers activity, it'd be very interesting to see what it brought up on Paddock.

One thing interesting with both of these sources of income is that you can easily launder money through them. I wondered about how he got the capital to buy properties in the first place, but the preferred method of laundering through real estate is to take out a mortgage and then pay it before it comes to full terms. It's also easily done through funneling money through tenants rental payments and he had many rental properties.

Here's the interesting bit The easiest way to launder money through casinos is with sports betting. The IRS requires reports of payouts over $10k, unless they come from sportsbooks with rural satellites that do under $1m/yr. An unusual regulation to close this loophole was recently brought up in October 16. What was unusual is that a casino licensee brought the regulation up. The licensee was Mesquite Gaming who operates Casa Blanca and Virgin River, both of which Paddock frequented. Was Mesquite Gaming mad about missing out on extra revenue from their customer Paddock? Might be a stretch but I thought it was interesting.

Paddock's Father

This seemed really odd to me, but maybe I just need to take my tinfoil hat off already.

  • Patrick Paddock was jailed for bank robbery in 1960
  • He escaped federal prison and robbed another bank in San Francisco in 1968, and was described as psychopathic and dangerous on the FBI most wanted list
  • 10 years later he was found and arrested when his picture was printed in a newspaper
  • He served one year in jail and got out on parole
  • In 1987 he was hit with racketeering charges and got off with no jail time and a fine of either $100k or $623k (I saw articles mentioning both numbers and don't know which is accurate)

It seems very odd to me that an escaped, psychopathic, serial bank robber that made the FBI most wanted list only served 1 year when he was finally caught. With a record like that, how did he get off with only a fine when he was caught again for racketeering? How did he have the money to pay those fines?

This might be a stretch buuuuut... The time Paddock's father escaped was during Hoover's reign as director of the FBI. Is it possible he did some kind of work for them and in return got helped out with future sentencing?

Does any of this seem weird to you guys lol? Sorry if I'm way off here, I'm new to this conspiracy stuff.
