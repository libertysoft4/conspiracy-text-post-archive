Who else here is an intellectually honest pizzagate skeptic?

6  2017-10-09 by procgen

I thought it might be helpful to quell the constant shill accusations by letting this sub see that there are many of us who have digested all of the evidence that we could find and remain unconvinced that the Podestas/Alefantis/Comet Pizza are involved in any way with child pornography or human trafficking. Please note that I am not saying it’s impossible - of course it’s possible. I’ve often been surprised by the things that seemingly warm-hearted people have done. But none of the emails, Instagram posts, or ‘creepy’ art have convinced me that something nefarious is going on, and I truly believe it’s unjust to spread a narrative that these people are monsters based on such flimsy & circumstantial (at best) evidence. It frightens me because it would be so easy to ruin the lives of innocent people if that’s all it took to damn someone in the public imagination. And that is precisely why I don’t want to ignore this. I don’t want to see our curiosity weaponized by anyone. And I want people to see that, yes, conspiracy theorists can be skeptics too, and that to defend someone accused of vile crimes is not equivalent to defending those crimes. I’m also not saying that people should stop looking critically at things. What I’m wary of is the fact that so many users become entirely convinced by relatively weak evidence and then begin pushing the narrative that person X is certainly guilty of crime Y, and that if anyone objects then they are part of the problem. That’s absurd! And it’s very dangerous, since it pollutes our discourse and makes it so much more difficult for people to see truth for themselves.
