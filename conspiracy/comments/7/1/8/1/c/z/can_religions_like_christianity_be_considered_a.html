Anyway, I think that what you're digging at here is ultimately something that will give an odd answer. Let's analyze the actors (writers and/or oral prophets) that, as you say, "brought the conspiracy to life." What motives would they have had? Deceit for power gain? Honest mistake? Metaphor for explaining things that were not understood? (N.B. assuming "false") Who can really guess? It's rather hard to discern what that motive might have been without knowing the individuals involved. And this is where a lot of people stop because the next argument gets into the worldview differences that arise from questions like these.

Whether or not these motives are "true," however, it turns up another interesting set of questions: We can analyze why such a thing as this claim was even possible. Why would any shyster think a "god" might be a good con game? Why would anyone ever believe in such a thing? Why could such a thing as a conscious entity be proposed to explain the mysterious? And here is where we get into evolution and psychology. Evolution has equipped humans with a mechanism to detect and observe agents. That mechanism functions even if the entity in question isn't a biological entity. Drawn eyes, for example, generate the illusion of being watched.

Well aware of the possibility of deceit, many people are content to assume it's all just a mistake. But what, specifically, is being described as an agent by people that believe in God? We know, from the description, that God is called "the creator" which implies that "reality itself" is the consequence of this being's action. It's worth noting that evolution and psychology are side-effects of reality. So, the proposition is an "entity that causes reality being permitted to be observed as an agent by creatures in reality." That's where it becomes an odd kind of "chicken and egg" situation. If we suppose such a thing were merely a human invention, humans can only do what reality permits. Some humans, somehow, have come to understand that God causes reality. Eventually, humans recognized that the only reason that humans would even be able to arrive at such a concept was that reality caused them to do such a thing. So, is it false to say that God inspired such a thing if reality is the effect of God? If we assume false, how would such a thing arise. If we assume true, it reveals an "infinite recursion" issue that resembles what happens when we ask what the "first cause" of the universe is. And, if we take the position of people who understand the belief system, it will be that our understanding is an imperfect reflection of the actual reality.

But, amongst other people there's an assumption that non-biological entities like that aren't actually valid agents to be described in such terms. So, they berate the religious for believing in such things and claim they are fairy tales. But, either way, human psychology and evolution have caused humanity to somehow introduce a meme that reflects the reality of the universe itself as a personification. Some humans are evidently more comfortable interfacing with reality that way than others are, however...
