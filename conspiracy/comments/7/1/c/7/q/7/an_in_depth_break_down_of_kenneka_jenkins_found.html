Alcohol is pretty nasty when it comes to cold weather. Normally without the influence of alcohol your body when subjected to cold temps will start closing off blood flow to your extremities. The effect is you feel colder as your extremities are getting colder, but it protects your core temp where all your organs and vital stuff is. If you stay in the cold too long you'll lose extremities like toes, fingers, and ears, but you'll stay alive. Assuming you don't stay out even longer and die.

With alcohol in your system your body doesn't stop sending blood to your extremities. So if you are way drunk you might not notice yourself go from warm to hypothermia, and once there you can actually start feeling warm again.
