1  eliasv 2017-12-01

Videos purported to show this are always just the result of compression artefacts. If you are familiar with these things it just seems so obvious to see.

Many image and video compression reencodes colour as hue/saturation/brightness values since brightness is the most important and the colour can be compressed way harder. This often results in colours getting messed up for small details.

Also the lossy part of compression affects different parts of an image differently and the effective resolution is different for different areas depending on how neatly the compression scheme can capture certain textures etc. This means that for videos where things are moving around, if they are heavily compressed then details which are just about on the edge of the effective resolution are liable to pop in and out.

These problems affect all parts of an image, people just notice it more with eyes because our brains are wired to be really good at recognising patterns and differences in faces.
