TL;DR: Take your height above the ground, multiply by 2 * PI, then divide by 24. This will give you the speed the ground will rotate faster than you per hour. It works for any units (Inches, Meters, Miles, etc.).

Yes it does... kinda.

If you imagine yourself in contact with the earth, you rotate once around the earth every day. As you travel upward, you keep that horizontal velocity (after all, what would have sped you up or slowed you down), but you're tracing a larger circle. That means you'll start lagging behind the ground below you.

Fortunately, it's not hard to find out how much you're lagging behind. Every foot you rise up, you gain 2 * PI feet in the circle you're tracing (about 6.25). That's the size difference in the circle you trace in 24 hours, which means you'll be that far behind after a day at that altitude. So, you can divide that amount by 24 to find the difference in speed per hour.
