Yuval Harari has an interesting theory that universal religion (i.e. religions that are believed to apply to all people universally, not just to a group/locality) is the third "universal imagined order" after the monetary order and the imperial order. An imagined order is a cultural tool that enables more effective cooperation between people outside of their immediate family/tribe and facilitates the creation of a more specialized society. Basically, human beings have an innate tendency to prefer to associate and cooperate only with those who are members of their "tribe." When two people share a common currency, live in the same empire (or state, nation, etc), or believe in the same religion, they recognize each other as being in the same tribe, even if they have many other differences. This recognition facilitates the creation of enormously complex and specialized organizations which can wield great power and influence (e.g. the Catholic church).

I suspect very few religions actually begin as conspiracies (except Scientology for example). It seems logical to me that cultural tools that empower are an emergent property of human intelligence, and effective cultural tools should become more prevalent over time, with more effective cultural tools supplanting less effective ones.

All imagined orders have the potential to empower the people who believe in them, but they also have the potential to empower an individual or select group of individuals, who use that power to exert control over that group and/or others. Religion possibly has the most potential for this than any other imagined order, because religion touches on the fundamental aspects of human belief and motivation, such as morality and the meaning of life. This makes them a natural target for those who would seek power for themselves.
