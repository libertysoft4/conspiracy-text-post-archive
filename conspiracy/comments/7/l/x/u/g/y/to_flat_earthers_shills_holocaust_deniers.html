1  Vietoris 2017-12-24

What about a ball filled with air? Why does it want to reach the surface of water if pressed down?

Sometimes, there are more than one force acting on the same object. How is that difficult to understand ?

Buoyancy is a force created by a gradient of pressure in a medium. A ball of air is pulled towards the center of the Earth due to gravity, but is also pushed by the gradient of pressure in water. As this gradient of pressure is in the opposite direction as gravity, the buoyancy force opposes the force of gravity.

As a ball of air is very light, the force of gravity is pretty small. However the ball of air has a large volume, so the buoyancy force is quite strong. Hence, in this case, buoyancy is stronger than gravity and hence the ball is going towards the surface.

It's all explainable with density and bouancy. Up is up and down is down.

No, it's not. Here are two example where "up is up" and "down is down" are no longer relevant when talking about buoyancy.

If you put a ball of air inside water in 0G (like in a parabolic flight), then the ball of air will stay still with respect to the water. That's because in 0g, the gradient of pressure disappears, and if there is no gradient the buoyancy force also disappears.

If you create a different gradient of pressure than the one created by gravity, then buoyancy will not push/pull vertically. For example, if you put an helium balloon in an accelerating car, then there will be an horizontal gradient of pressure (in addition to the vertical gradient created by gravity), and hence the helium balloon will be pushed horizontally by this new component of buoyancy.
