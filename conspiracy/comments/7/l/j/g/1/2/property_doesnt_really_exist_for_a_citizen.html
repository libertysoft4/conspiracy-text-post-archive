Property doesn't really exist for a citizen

12  2017-12-22 by GoTomArrow

Just a thought/thought experiment that came to my mind today.

You don't really own anything as a citizen. How could you. There is no way you can realistically protect what you "own", which I think is something that is a significant component of owning something.

If I pluck an apple from a tree, I could be said to own it. But if someone comes along who is stronger than me and just takes it away from me, am I really the owner? Or am I just someone who believes in an illusion?

Though, the one strong enough to take it away AND keep it, I think that one can realistically be called an owner. That person really owns the apple.

My point is, I think that we don't really own anything. In reality, it's the government that owns everything. And our role in it? Our role is just to be temporary caretakers of it. We are, at the mercy of our rulers, granted the liberty to manage our so-called property.

But if the government decides that we should no longer have it, there is not a single thing we can do about it. What does that mean? It means we don't own it. We have absolutely zero means to stop a government from taking "our" stuff away. So in no realistic way is it actually "ours". In my opinion.

And when someone steals from you? In most places the only thing you can do is go tell the government about it. To inform them that someone has taken the governments property away from you, the manager of that government property, despite you having been promised that you would be the manager. In other words, the only thing you can do is go cry to momma and hope she fixes it.

So the only person that can realistically make sure that you get to keep "your" property is the government. You aren't allowed to take care of that yourself. Then you would be considered a vigilante, or worse.

Again, the only reasonable conclusion imo is that it's not really "yours" in any meaningful sense.

Rather, it all comes down to a trick of language/our ideology. Our ideology has invented the term "ownership" and delusionally distorted it to something that will make a citizen feel good, motivated and effective. The idea that they have power over their "property". Yet of course they don't. They only borrow the government's property, and then they only get to use the government's power to "protect" their so-called property.

In the very same sense it could be said that the government owns us as people. When it comes down to it, we have absolutely zero autonomy. The only autonomy we have is an illusion granted to us to make us more efficient and motivated, and of course very limited and with strict constraints and conditions.

This ties into something else that I've been thinking for a while ... when people are motivated to whine about their "human rights" and stuff like that - I always have to cringe a little. Why? Because it's pathetic and has zero dignity. The only reason you can cry about your "rights" and your so-called "freedom" is because the government allows you this illusion of "rights" and "freedoms". Yet while you are led to believe these rights are somehow inherent to who you are, they are really much more inherent to what the government wants you to be.

To cry "I am free! I have rights!" is to assert your freedom by referring to your slavery (your submission to the concept of "rights" that you have been indoctrinated with and made to rely upon since your birth). And that's just ridiculous. It's like screaming "I have submitted myself completely to the belief system you demanded me to submit to, and now I expect you to abide by it!" It's an exclamation of complete powerlessness. If you aren't powerful enough to control your own thoughts and belief systems and are required (with no exception) to submit to what you're told, how can you possibly delude yourself into thinking that you have any power whatsoever to change how you are being treated? When the only reason you even dare to speak up is that you've been told that that is expected of you?

But maybe that's the whole point ... one is so powerless and in such utter submission that one is not even allowed to recognize that one is not free. One is so much a slave that one is powerless to NOT believe in one's own freedom. Powerless to NOT protest and cry wolf and demand one's "rights". It's not you demanding your rights because you have some - it's you throwing a temper tantrum because that is what the puppet player wants you to do.
