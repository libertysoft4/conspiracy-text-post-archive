Privacy concerns over new Google app that matches people's faces to famous works of art as critics warn it is creating a detailed 'facial recognition

129  2018-01-16 by LizMcIntyre

The new Google "doppelganger" art app matches a selfie you upload to famous historical paintings. The idea is to show you faces in historical paintings that look a lot like you.

Google told MailOnline that uploaded images aren't used for any other purpose and are deleted after the match is made.

But this assurance of deletion hasn't quelled concerns over facial biometrics. Could this app be for "machine learning" training?
