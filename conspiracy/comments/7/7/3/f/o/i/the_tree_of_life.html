The solar winged god above the tree is the Sumerian god Shemash. He [Shemash] is usually depicted as an old man with a long beard whose shoulders emanate rays of light but is also represented as the solar disc or, in the Neo-Assyrian Period (c. 912-612 BCE), as a disc with wings. The Sumerians are showing reverence for the sun, because to them, and ancient mankind's perspective, the sun's rays are giving them and making the tree of life grow.
